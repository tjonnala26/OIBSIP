About

Atal Bihari Vajpayee (1924-2018) was a prominent Indian politician and statesman. He served as the Prime Minister of India three times, first in 1996 for a short duration, then from 1998 to 2004. Vajpayee was a founding member of the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) and played a pivotal role in shaping its ideology. He was known for his oratory skills and ability to build coalitions across the political spectrum.

During his tenure as Prime Minister, India conducted nuclear tests in 1998, asserting its nuclear capabilities. Vajpayee also focused on economic reforms, infrastructure development, and improving India's relations with neighboring countries. He was a highly respected leader known for his inclusive approach and commitment to secularism. Vajpayee's leadership left a lasting impact on Indian politics, and he is remembered as a visionary statesman. He passed away on August 16, 2018.
